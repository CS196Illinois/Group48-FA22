Relevant history
Ancient Egyptian civilization lasted for almost 3000 years from 3100 BC to 300 BC
The Nile River is an integral part of Egypt: The fertile banks of the Nile cultivate life
Ancient Egyptians were one of the first civilizations to develop a writing system, hieroglyphs.
Hieroglyphs are made up of a mixture of pictures and symbols called glyphs
Egypt was ruled by pharaohs (kings and queens of dynasties) during their dynastic period
Ancient Egyptian was divided into the Old Kingdom, the Middle Kingdom, and the New Kingdom

Important inventions/discoveries
Egypt is home to some of the most renowned architectural traditions. Significant architectural locations include the ancient city Memphis, the necropolis, the Pyramids of Giza, tombs and ruins in the Valley of the Queens, and many more
The creation of one of the first writing systems, Hieroglyphs
Papyrus sheets, makeup, advances in medicine, toothpaste, and sundials were some other developments made by Egyptians

Daily life
Family is a very important social aspect in Egypt
Foreign influences such as Western cultures have heavily influenced urban tastes in art, literature, cuisine, clothing, etc.
Egyptians celebrate a number of holidays such as Coptic Christmas Day, Labor Day, National Day, Eid al-Adha, and others
The arts are an important aspect of Egyptian society. There is a great emphasis placed upon literature, dance, music, and more
Contemporary sports include basketball, swimming, wrestling, etc

Religion
Egyptians believe that there is life after death 
They would bury dead people in tombs with everything they might need for the afterlife
The state religion of Egypt is Islam
Egyptians have worshiped hundreds of deities such as Osiris, Isis, Horus, Seth, Anubis, Hathor, and more (Egyptians are polytheistic)
These gods and goddesses often appear in aspects of Egyptian life
